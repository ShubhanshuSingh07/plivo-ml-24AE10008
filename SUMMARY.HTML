DELIVERABLE: SUMMARY

End-of-Turn detection from prosody alone

A causal, CPU-only classifier that scores each annotated pause with p_eot—the probability the speaker has finished—using nothing but hand-built acoustic features and gradient-boosted trees. No pretrained models, no external data, English and Hindi trained jointly.

pause_start

■ OBSERVABLE PREFIX | 1.5 S TRAILING WINDOW | THE CAUSAL CUT
NEVER READ

The waveform is truncated once, at the top of extract_causal_features(), and the untruncated array is deleted from scope. Everything downstream including the per-turn normalisation statistics - physically cannot see past the cut.

1. Methodology

Causality

For a pause at pause_start, the only input is y[: int (pause_start*sr)]. Three consequences worth stating explicitly:

  • Pitch tracking is re-run per pause: pyin applies Viterbi smoothing across the whole array it is given, so tracking the full turn once and slicing afterwards would leak the future backwards. F0 is estimated on each causal prefix separately.
  • Normalisation is causal: Per-turn z-stats for pitch and energy come from the prefix only, so early pauses are normalised against less history as they would be at inference.
  • pause_end and label are dropped at load time in predict.py. Pause duration is the single most predictive feature in this task and is not available to a live system at the moment of decision; it is not used.

65 features total. Language is not a feature - the model is trained on both folders jointly and cannot condition on locale, so it must find contours that generalise.
